My personal interests

One of my most sincere and enduring interests has been the study of the Spanish language.
Like many other Mormons, I signed up to represent the church as a missionary, and they told
me I would be teaching our message in the Spanish language. By the end of my 2-year tenure as a
missionary, I was hooked on Hispanic Linguistics; it became an obsession for the better part of a
decade, and though I have other focuses these days, the flame will never die out.

Another interest of mine is the 1993 Dodge Cummins 3/4 ton pickup truck. What especially captures
my attention is the sheer, raw simplicity of the powertrain. The engine doesn't need any
electricity to run. If you can get it spinning (clutch-bump), it will run. The other aspect of the
vehicle that I love is how long-lived the engine is. It is normal for the Cummins 6BT to last for
750,000 miles.

My final significant interest could be called "manufactured". I didn't initially care for software,
and in fact I began to study it for financial reason (I watned the paycheck). However as I dove deeper
into the subversive world of web development, as well as the intricate field of software optimization,
I discovered a certain power to software that was alluring. I never realized that computers were initially
meant to be owned and operated by the user, not borrowed from the manufacturer like they are nowadays.
